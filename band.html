Microsoft Band

The Microsoft Band is a small "wearable computing" device worn on a user's wrist. While its form is more of a band in lines with a fitness band like Fitbits and the Nike Fuelbands, as a wearable computing device it also competes with smartwatches like the Apple Watch. Also known simply as "Band," the Microsoft Band offers a raft of features, starting with the ability to view text messages, incoming calls to your smartphone, voicemail notifications, Facebook updates, and new tweets. The Microsoft Health service is another significant component of the Microsoft Band, and its functionality includes 24-hour heart rate tracking, sleep tracking, a built-in GPS sensor for tracking runs and other workouts, a UV monitor for monitoring sun exposure, a cloud service for storing and analyzing health and fitness data, and an app for smartphones and tablets for viewing Microsoft Health insights.
